The political hub of the region, Bengaluru is a world apart from the rest of the state and in many ways India’s most Westernized urban centre. Once a sleepy cantonment, the charming, verdant “Garden City” of just over 600,0000 people at Independence has been completely transformed by the technology boom into both a trendy, racy business hub and a bustling, smog-choked megalopolis of nearly twelve million, perhaps the fastest growing city in India.

H o w T o R e a c h B a n g a l o r e

By Air:


Bengaluru International Airport which is called as Kempegowda International Airport(KIA), is 35km Northeast of the city in Devanahalli which is located on Hyderabad road. It’s one of the most busiest airport in India and the most spacious in the country, with top-notch facilities. You can get into the city by Ola, Uber or any other private Airport Taxi or efficient a/c Vayu Vajra bus operated specially by BMTC wich runs fo KIA to major Bus Stations in te city. KSRTC also runs direct FlyBus services from the airport to Mysuru, Madikeri and Tirupati, plus an overnight service to Kundapur on the coast, via Mangaluru and Udupi. It's a very well connected International Airport which handles flights from various major carriers like Air India, Emirates, British Airways, Qantas, Singapore Airlines connecting major cities of the world and almost all the cities of out country.

By Rail:


Bangalore is very well connected through Railways from various parts of our country. There are many railway stations in the city to handle the traffic of many trains, KSR bangalore being the main one located in the heart of the city, connecting almost all the major cities of India and many parts of karnataka as well, the other being Yeshwantpur Jn, Banaswadi, Bangalore Cantt., Krishnarajapuram stations. All the stations fall under SWR division. There is very well connectivity from these stations to all parts of the city as well, as all the stations are located in the city itself. Added to that, there are also many small stations like Kengeri, Devanahalli as well. One can take BMTC buses, hire autos and also cabs are affordable places. But one has to be careful about the traffic while moving in the city.

By Bus:


Bangalore is very well connected by Bus from all the major cities located around Bangalore. There is very good bus connectivity between Bangalore and many major cities like Chennai, Trivandrum, Pune, Mumbai, Ahmedabad, Vishakapatnam and also even cities like Ahmedabad, Jodhpur , Jalore etc. Many private buses also operate buses overnight and day services from Bangalore which include Volvo a/c, Sleeper and Non a/c servcies too which also give a flexibility of choosing boarding point from many points around the city. Major operators include VRL, SRS, Neeta, Jabbar ,etc. Also the buses operated by Karnataka RTC are also very well maintained and awarded as the best RTC in our country. It operates all types of services like the normal ones, a/c and Non a/c Sleeper servcies, Volvo services called as Airavata Club Class and newly introduced premium a/c sleeper servicecalled Ambari Dream Class. It operates buses to almost every corner of our state and many interstate services to Pondicherry, Chennai, Madurai, Kochi, Waynad, Panaji, Mumbai, Hyderabad, Vijaywada and many other major cities. To avoid crowding and help passengers buses plying to various regions are placed at many bus terminals in the city. Buses heading towards like Hubli, Maharashtra, Goa starts from Terminal 1 of Kempegowda Bus Station also called as Majestic located at the heart of the city. Buses towards Mangalore and other western parts starts from Terminal 2 of Kempegowda Bus Station. Those towards Andhra pradesh, Telangana and Tamilnadu starts from Shantinagar Bus Station. Buses towards Mysore, Waynad, Kerala starts from satellite bus station in Kengeri. Apart from these buses of other RTCs of Telangana, Maharashtra, Tamilnadu, Kerala also offer servcies to and fro Bangalore.

By Road:


Bnaglore being one of the most Important city of South India is very well conncted by roads. It is one of the major cities in the Golden Quadrilateral. The NH4 running between Mumbai to Chennai runs between the city, NH7 the longest National Highway of India runing between Varanasi and Kanyakumari passes through the city. Also there are many other NHs, SHs, connecting Bangalore to surrounding cities. To avoid congestion in the city and avoid traffic, NICE Road has been been very beautifully around the city. The 6-lane roads are very well maintained which collects though give a great service. One can easily get out of the city or ply from one corner to other easily within no time. Being one of the busiest city in the world, traffic is one of the major problem. Many flyovers, main roads do exists in bangalore, but traffic becomes major problem specially during weekends and evenings. So, it's better to plan accordingly. Reaching Bangalore by road is not a great deal at all. Major well maintained roads to exists for reaching Bangalore from all all the surrounding places.
